## Design Context

### Users
Agencies building white-label document pipelines and ops teams at mid-size companies needing branded, professional documents on demand. They generate invoices, reports, proposals, and branded PDFs for clients or their own organizations. Professional context — desktop, business hours, results-oriented. They care about reliability and output quality, not flashy tech.

### Brand Personality
Precise, personalized, professional. DocForge feels like a high-end studio tool — not a startup, not a dev toy. The document equivalent of a Swiss watch: everything works exactly as expected, with craft visible in the details.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark throughout (slate-950 base — keep this). Reference: Google Antigravity — large bold typography, editorial whitespace, high contrast, minimal but deeply considered composition. The design should feel authoritative and refined. Anti-references: anything that looks like AI-generated SaaS (purple-to-blue gradients, glassmorphism, emoji icons above feature cards, stat grids with big numbers). No generic dark SaaS template energy.

### Design Principles
1. Typography does the heavy lifting — one beautiful, unexpected display font paired with a refined body font. Follow the impeccable font selection procedure fully. Do NOT use any font on the `reflex_fonts_to_reject` list.
2. Whitespace signals confidence — don't pack features into a grid, let things breathe
3. Precision in every detail — exact spacing, considered color, nothing random
4. Documents are the product — visual language should evoke high-quality print craft, exactness
5. Dark means refined, not gamer — darkness here is like a museum at night, not a gaming setup
